Target Audience

  • Residents in the area concerned about the weather
  • Homeschool parents with kids ages 6-12 who want to do a weather unit for science class
  • High School students considering meteorology as a possible career

Personas

Melanie Mills

Photo by Alexander Dummer on Unsplash

Melanie Mills is a 31 year old homeschool mom of 4 kids Molly (6), Marky (4), Mimi (3), and Mo (1). She uses The Weather Duck website primarily as a resource for science lessons. Her oldest child, Molly, loves playing educational games on the website after they finish their lessons for the day. Melanie is also the Park Day coordinator for her local homeschool group and uses the website to plan park days when the weather is suitable.

Janie Storm

Photo by Noah Stillman on Unsplash

Janie Storm is a 17-year-old High School student at the local high school. She is absolutely fascinated with everything related to the weather and wants to be a meteorologist when she grows up. Janie uses the website to learn everything she can about meteorology and likes to keep track of the weather every day so she can practice doing weather forecasts. Sometimes, just for fun, she likes to go out in bad weather and pretend she is giving live weather updates.

Scenarios

  • Is it a good day to play at the park?
  • When will the rain stop so I can go for a run?
  • Why is it snowing in May?
  • How do clouds form?
  • How do you know where to point when using a green screen to do the weather?
